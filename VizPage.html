Remittances in the Northern Triangle go towards

non-essential items!

709 households in the World food program study gave consent about receiving remittances. Of these only 628 reported consistent information about how they spend their remittances.

The expense categories that were reported in the study include:
  • Food
  • Health
  • Covid-19 Expenses
  • Education
  • Utilities
  • Clothing
  • Housing construction
  • Housing rental
  • Home purchase
  • Purchase of Agricultural Inputs
  • Business Ventures
  • Savings
  • Financial Commitments of the Migrant
  • Migration Debts and
  • other debts
The top 5 expense categories reported by most households are Food, Utilities, Health, Clothing and Education
556 of the 628 households reported that they spend part of their income on Food
Utilities were the second largest expense in terms of the number of families that reported this type of expense
Moreover, 241 households reported spending part of their remittance income on health care expenses
Only 14 of the 628 households reported that they save some of the remittance money
